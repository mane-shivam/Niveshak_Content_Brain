## üéØ FRAMEWORK CATEGORIES

Frameworks should fall into these categories:

### 1. **Metric Frameworks**
Proprietary metrics or novel ways to interpret standard metrics.  
*Examples: GMV vs. Revenue divergence, ESOP dilution velocity, Working capital cycle compression*

### 2. **Regime Frameworks**
Mental models for understanding how market/policy regimes shape outcomes.  
*Examples: Rate regime impact on asset-light models, Liquidity regime vs. quality premium*

### 3. **Governance Frameworks**
Patterns for evaluating management quality and incentive alignment.  
*Examples: Pledging velocity as stress signal, Related-party transaction growth analysis*

### 4. **Sector-Specific Frameworks**
Industry-specific lenses for evaluating business models.  
*Examples: Food delivery unit economics, D2C customer acquisition cost efficiency*

### 5. **Cross-Sector Pattern Frameworks**
Universal patterns that repeat across industries.  
*Examples: Subsidy-to-dominance playbook, Platform economics at scale*

## üéì FRAMEWORK COMPOUNDING STRATEGY

**Goal:** Build an interconnected web of frameworks that reference and reinforce each other.

**How:**
1. **Layering** ‚Äî When introducing Framework B, reference Framework A if related
2. **Integration** ‚Äî Combine frameworks for multi-dimensional analysis
3. **Evolution** ‚Äî Refine frameworks as we learn (document changes in Notes)
4. **Teaching** ‚Äî Create dedicated posts that go deep on high-performing frameworks

**Example of Compounding:**
> "In our Operating Leverage framework, we showed how margin expansion accelerates in growth phases. Now apply that to the Regime Framework: in a high-rate regime, companies with negative operating leverage face compounding pressure."
